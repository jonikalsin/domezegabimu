The Meaning Behind The Song: Feeling Like The End by Joji

As a music journalist, I always find joy in dissecting the lyrics and unraveling the hidden meanings behind the songs that resonate deeply with listeners. Today, I want to delve into the hauntingly beautiful track, “Feeling Like The End” by Joji. This song, with its introspective lyrics and mesmerizing melody, has captivated the hearts of many, including myself.

A Personal Connection

I first heard this song on a rainy evening, as I sat alone in my room, contemplating the swirling thoughts that seemed to consume my mind. It was one of those moments where the world felt heavy, and every emotion was amplified. And in that vulnerable state, I stumbled upon this song.

From the moment the first chord struck, I was enraptured. The hauntingly tender vocals of Joji echoed through the room, reaching deep within my soul. It was as if the lyrics were pulled straight from my own tumultuous emotions. The emotions that often make us feel like we are spiraling towards an inevitable end.

Listening to “Feeling Like The End” felt like a cathartic experience, as though someone had taken the time to understand my inner turmoil and craft a song around it. The lyrics painted vivid pictures of despair, longing, and the struggle to find hope amidst darkness.

The Power of Vulnerability

One of the striking aspects of this song is Joji’s ability to convey vulnerability. His raw and honest delivery makes you feel every ounce of pain, every flicker of hope, and every desperate plea for salvation. The vulnerability in his voice is a reminder that it’s okay to embrace our own vulnerabilities, as they are a testament to our humanity.

The lyrics reflect this vulnerability, exploring themes of loneliness, heartbreak, and the fear of losing oneself. Lines like “I’m drowning inside my own head” and “I don’t wanna wake up lonely” resonate deeply with anyone who has struggled with their inner demons.

Symbolism Through Sound

Beyond the profound lyrics, the production of “Feeling Like The End” creates a haunting atmosphere that perfectly complements the emotional weight of the song. The soft piano chords, distant echoes, and subtle electronic elements evoke a sense of desolation, as if we are traversing through a desolate landscape.

The song’s slow tempo and deliberate pacing allow the lyrics to take center stage, emphasizing their impact and allowing listeners to fully immerse themselves in the emotional journey that Joji embarks upon.

The Deeper Meaning

While “Feeling Like The End” may appear to be a song about despair and hopelessness on the surface, there is a glimmer of resilience and strength embedded within. It speaks to the universal experience of navigating through the darkest moments of our lives and finding the will to keep going.

The chorus, with its powerful repetition of “I don’t ever wanna feel like / I’m feeling like the end,” showcases Joji’s refusal to succumb to despair. It is a declaration of self-preservation and a reminder that even in our most challenging moments, there is always a flicker of light to guide us forward.

A Song That Inspires

“Feeling Like The End” is more than just a song; it is an intimate exploration of the human experience. It reminds us that we are not alone in our struggles and that there is beauty in embracing our vulnerabilities.

As I continue to listen to this song, I am reminded that even in the darkest of times, there is always a glimmer of hope. A hope that propels us to keep moving forward, to push through the pain, and ultimately find solace in the journey itself.

So, the next time you find yourself feeling lost or overwhelmed, I encourage you to listen to “Feeling Like The End” by Joji. Allow yourself to be vulnerable, to feel deeply, and find solace in the realization that we are all on this journey together.
