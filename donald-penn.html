Donald Penn – Professional Life

After being released by the Minnesota Vikings in 2006, Tampa Bay signed Donald Penn. He signed a one-year, $2.792 million deal with the Buccaneers in April 2009. He signed a six-year, $43 million deal on July 30, 2010. Penn was selected as an alternate to the 2011 Pro Bowl, replacing Green Bay Packers tackle Chad Clifton.

Similarly, he scored his second career touchdown against the Miami Dolphins on November 11, 2013. On March 13, 2014, he was released. He was signed to a two-year, $9.6 million contract by the Oakland Raiders on March 18, 2014, and scored his third career touchdown as a qualifying offensive lineman. He was re-signed by the Raiders in March 2016 to a two-year, $14 million deal. In 2016, Penn was called to his second Pro Bowl.
