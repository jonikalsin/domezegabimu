Canadian Actress Marie-Marguerite Sabongui's Personal Life! Know About Her Career, Net Worth, Relati

Marie-Marguerite Sabongui is a Canadian-Egyptian-American actress who got worldwide popularity after entering the cinema industry. Her debut character was Allegra Zane in Galidor: Defenders of the Outer Dimension. Moreover, she was named an Inaugural Sundance and Youtube New Voices Fellow in 2016 for the series The Mission, which she created along with her siblings. 

The 155 cm tall celeb writes and produces in addition to acting and has won honors for her theatrical direction. She continues to work on advocacy and impact production on a global scale.

Bio And Early Life

Marie-Marguerite Sabongui was born on 28 December 1983 in Montreal, Quebec, Canada, to Wadid Sabongui and Leila Rizkalla. She grew up with her four siblings: Patrick Sabongui, Rami Joseph, Amir Georges, Camille Anthony Sabongui. 

Sabongui is the sister-in-law of Kyra Zagorsky, married to her brother Patrick. Besides, she is trilingual and can speak French, English, and Arabic fluently. She learned dancing at a young age and has worked in theater.

Regarding her education qualifications, she is a graduated with BA from McGill University in Cultural Studies. Later, she did her Masters' in Environmental Science and Policy at Columbia University.

Marie-Marguerite Sabongui Career 

Marie-Marguerite Sabongui worked for environmental and social justice throughout her acting career. She was the face of Rogers Telecom's My5 campaign in Canada from 2010 to 2012 through the Vancouver Olympics. 

She also went to the Montreal Conservatoire de Danse, the Neighborhood Playhouse School of Theatre, and the Upright Citizens' Brigade for her training. Moreover, she was one of the first Canadians to receive Al Gore's Inconvenient Truth addresses training.
