The teammate of Lionel Messi at Inter Miami whose family is wealthier than the Argentine star and Cristiano Ronaldo

The story of Leonardo Campana is quite distinctive. The Ecuadorian striker’s family fortune is reputedly worth $1 billion, surpassing the wealth of even Messi and Ronaldo.

In Ecuador, Campana’s grandmother Isabel Noboa is well recognised for being a successful businesswoman, and his grandfather Isidro Romero is a well-known businessman, politician, and sports leader.

Romero played a crucial part in the development of Barcelona SC’s stadium, which they named in his honour. Strangely, Campana’s first career goal and Messi’s magic with Argentina were both observed at the Estadio Monumental Isidro Romero Carbo. Pablo Campana, Leonardo’s father, was also a professional tennis player for Ecuador in the 1996 Olympics.

At the age of 24, he gave up playing sports but found success in the business world, rising to the position of Minister of Foreign Trade and Investments. More well-known people are in his family tree. While Campana’s uncle Daniel Noboa is vying for the presidency of Ecuador, his great-uncle Alvaro Noboa is a well-known businessman throughout the Americas.

According to reports, the 23-year-old forward makes $550,000 a year, making him one of Inter Miami’s highest-paid players. Campana can be proud of being a teammate of one of the greatest players in history in addition to inheriting a legacy of commercial success.
